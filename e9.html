Object Design

Mar 16, 2025

Entry 9

The animations for our game are coming along really well. Caithlin has been doing all of the animation so far. I suggested adding a subtle ‘bobbing’ motion to the collectible power up items to make them stand out more and feel alive within the environment. It’s a small touch, but I think details like that can make a big difference to the overall aesthetic and player experience. It’s already starting to look really polished! Niamh also showed us the work she had been doing on the navigation of the player. She showed us a snippet of the game with walking sounds when the player moves the character and the player is now able to pick up the apple. Jane has also informed the team that she has been taking a C# course and will be ready to implement any code needed. This progress has reassured me that we will be able to finish our game on time. Niamh has also told us she has some of the background music completed, but we have not heard any of it yet. Despite past setbacks, this has given me more confidence that we’ll be able to complete the game on time.

That said, I’m still concerned about the room randomisation system. I haven’t received any updates from Luigi, and despite several attempts to reach out, I haven’t gotten any replies. This lack of communication is frustrating, especially as room generation is such a vital part of the gameplay. I’m unsure how far along he is, or if anything has been started at all. I feel like there is not much I can do when all communication attempts are ignored. I just hope Luigi is working on the room randomisation in his own time.

This week I began to work on the objects to decorate rooms I mentioned previously and the weapons needed. We plan on beginning the game with a magic wand but throughout the game the princess will be able to pick up different types of swords with varying levels of damage. We originally planned to include several swords with different abilities or levels of damage. However, due to time constraints and scope, we’ve decided to scale this back. Now, the player will begin the game with a magic wand and will have the opportunity to pick up one of two sword types: a basic one and a more ornate version with slightly higher damage. Jane was originally tasked with researching and implementing the attack system, but since she's still learning, we’ve simplified our approach to focus on what we know we can complete confidently.

This week, I also started working on environmental assets, such as furniture and other objects to bring each room to life. I’ve really enjoyed this process. It’s been fun thinking of creative ways to visually enhance the different spaces and strengthen the game’s fantasy aesthetic. Despite the setbacks and occasional communication issues, I’m starting to see our game really come together. It's exciting to see all the visual and gameplay elements slowly falling into place. I’m hopeful that, with continued focus and teamwork, we’ll be able to deliver something we’re all proud of.
